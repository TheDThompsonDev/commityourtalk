# Commit Your Talk - Curriculum

## Philosophy

**Commit Your Talk** is designed specifically for software engineers who want to become confident public speakers. We understand that many developers are introverts who find public speaking daunting. Our curriculum meets you where you are and helps you progress at your own pace.

## Core Principles

### 1. Progressive Skill Building
Start small with 30-second introductions, gradually building to full conference talks. Each step is achievable and builds on the last.

### 2. Safe Learning Environment
Practice in our Discord community where everyone is supportive and constructive. No judgment, only growth.

### 3. Tech-Focused Content
All examples, exercises, and scenarios are relevant to software engineering. Learn to explain code, present architectures, and demo technical concepts.

### 4. Real-World Application
Every lesson includes practical exercises that mirror real speaking situations: standups, tech talks, interviews, conferences, and leadership presentations.

### 5. Community Feedback
Receive constructive feedback from peers and experienced coaches. Learn from watching others and sharing your experiences.

## Learning Paths

### Path 1: Speaking Foundations (Beginner)
**Duration:** 6 weeks | **Target Audience:** Introverts taking their first steps

**Goal:** Break out of your shell and build foundational confidence

#### Lessons:
1. **Breaking the Ice: Your First Talk** (30 min)
   - 30-second self-introduction exercise
   - Understanding nervousness as normal
   - Building a supportive mindset

2. **Structuring Your Message** (45 min)
   - The 3-part structure: Hook â†’ Content â†’ Close
   - Applying structure to technical topics
   - Creating memorable openings and closings

3. **Managing Nervousness & Anxiety** (40 min)
   - Breathing techniques and physical relaxation
   - Power poses and confidence building
   - Mental reframing strategies

4. **Vocal Variety & Tone** (50 min)
   - Pace, pitch, and volume control
   - Emphasis and pausing for effect
   - Recording and self-assessment exercises

5. **Body Language Basics** (45 min)
   - Confident posture and stance
   - Natural gesturing techniques
   - Eye contact (in-person and virtual)

6. **Your First 3-Minute Tech Talk** (60 min)
   - Capstone: Present about your favorite dev tool
   - Receive community feedback
   - Reflect and set next goals

**Outcomes:**
- Overcome initial fear of speaking
- Deliver short, structured presentations
- Manage physical symptoms of nervousness
- Build confidence through small wins

### Path 2: The Conference Speaker (Intermediate)
**Duration:** 8 weeks | **Target Audience:** Developers ready to submit CFPs

**Goal:** Craft compelling talks and get accepted to conferences

#### Lessons:
1. **Crafting Your Core Message** (50 min)
   - Identifying your unique perspective
   - Turning experience into insights
   - Packaging ideas for conference audiences

2. **The Art of Storytelling** (55 min)
   - The hero's journey framework
   - Before/after transformations
   - Problem-solution narratives
   - Making technical content memorable

3. **Slide Design for Developers** (60 min)
   - Minimalist design principles
   - Code highlighting best practices
   - Visual hierarchy and readability
   - Tools: Figma, Canva, Keynote, PowerPoint

4. **Opening Strong, Closing Memorable** (45 min)
   - Hook techniques: questions, stories, statistics
   - Building anticipation and momentum
   - Crafting calls-to-action
   - Leaving lasting impressions

5. **Handling Q&A with Confidence** (50 min)
   - Techniques for tough questions
   - Admitting when you don't know
   - Managing hostile questioners
   - Turning questions into opportunities

6. **Writing a Winning CFP** (55 min)
   - Understanding conference selection processes
   - Writing compelling titles and abstracts
   - Highlighting value to the audience
   - Common mistakes to avoid

7. **Your 20-Minute Conference Talk** (90 min)
   - Capstone: Full conference-style presentation
   - Slides, delivery, and Q&A
   - Recorded for review and portfolio

**Outcomes:**
- Create and deliver 20-minute technical talks
- Design professional, engaging slides
- Handle Q&A with grace
- Submit compelling conference proposals

---

### Path 3: Technical Deep Dive (Advanced)
**Duration:** 6 weeks | **Target Audience:** Senior engineers explaining complex topics

**Goal:** Master explaining advanced technical concepts clearly

#### Lessons:
1. **Simplifying Complex Concepts** (50 min)
   - Using analogies and mental models
   - Progressive disclosure techniques
   - Adapting to audience technical levels
   - Examples: Kubernetes, distributed systems, algorithms

2. **Live Coding Without Disasters** (60 min)
   - Preparing scripts and backups
   - Handling errors gracefully
   - Narrating while coding
   - Keeping audiences engaged during demos

3. **Architecture Diagrams That Work** (55 min)
   - Progressive diagram building
   - Consistent notation systems
   - Highlighting key components
   - Tools: Excalidraw, Lucidchart, Miro

4. **Code Walkthroughs & Explanations** (50 min)
   - Highlighting without reading
   - Explaining intent vs. implementation
   - Pacing your explanations
   - Using annotations effectively

5. **Handling Technical Questions** (45 min)
   - Thinking on your feet
   - Admitting complexity honestly
   - Offering to follow up
   - Managing expert audiences

6. **Your Technical Deep Dive Presentation** (90 min)
   - Capstone: 30-minute architecture/system design talk
   - Include live demo or code walkthrough
   - Handle technical Q&A

**Outcomes:**
- Explain complex topics to mixed audiences
- Execute live coding demonstrations
- Create clear architecture diagrams
- Answer deep technical questions confidently

---

### Path 4: Leadership & Influence (Advanced)
**Duration:** 7 weeks | **Target Audience:** Engineering leaders and aspiring managers

**Goal:** Develop executive presence and persuasive communication

#### Lessons:
1. **Executive Presence for Engineers** (50 min)
   - Clarity, confidence, and gravitas
   - Communication patterns of technical leaders
   - Commanding the room without dominating
   - Authenticity vs. performance

2. **The Art of Persuasion** (55 min)
   - Aristotle's rhetorical triangle: Ethos, Pathos, Logos
   - Building credibility through evidence
   - Emotional connection in technical contexts
   - Logical argumentation structures

3. **Presenting to Executives** (60 min)
   - Framing technical decisions in business terms
   - Leading with outcomes, not implementation
   - Handling pushback and objections
   - Time management in high-stakes presentations

4. **Leading Technical Discussions** (50 min)
   - Facilitating architecture reviews
   - Managing debates and conflicts
   - Building consensus across teams
   - Moderating without dominating

5. **Inspiring & Motivating Teams** (55 min)
   - All-hands presentations
   - Team kickoff talks
   - Vision casting
   - Rallying people around missions

6. **Handling Organizational Politics** (50 min)
   - Delivering bad news diplomatically
   - Disagreeing with leadership constructively
   - Managing stakeholder expectations
   - Navigating difficult conversations

7. **Your Leadership Presentation** (90 min)
   - Capstone: Technical strategy or vision talk
   - Persuade stakeholders on a decision
   - Demonstrate executive presence

**Outcomes:**
- Command the room with executive presence
- Persuade and influence through communication
- Present technical decisions to non-technical audiences
- Inspire and motivate teams

## Progression Model

### Stage 1: Nervous Beginner (Weeks 1-6)
- **Mindset:** "I'm terrified of public speaking"
- **Skills:** Basic structure, nervousness management
- **Practice:** 30-second to 3-minute talks
- **Environment:** Supportive Discord community

### Stage 2: Emerging Speaker (Weeks 7-14)
- **Mindset:** "I can present, but conferences seem out of reach"
- **Skills:** Storytelling, slide design, Q&A handling
- **Practice:** 10-20 minute presentations
- **Environment:** Discord + maybe local meetups

### Stage 3: Confident Presenter (Weeks 15-20)
- **Mindset:** "I can explain complex topics clearly"
- **Skills:** Technical deep dives, live demos, architecture explanations
- **Practice:** 20-30 minute technical talks
- **Environment:** Conferences, internal tech talks

### Stage 4: Influential Leader (Weeks 21-27)
- **Mindset:** "I can inspire and persuade through communication"
- **Skills:** Executive presence, strategic communication, leadership
- **Practice:** Vision talks, strategic presentations
- **Environment:** All-hands, executive meetings, keynotes

## Community Guidelines

### Feedback Principles
1. **Be Kind:** We're all learning together
2. **Be Specific:** "Great job" doesn't help; actionable feedback does
3. **Be Constructive:** Point out strengths AND growth areas
4. **Be Encouraging:** Celebrate progress, no matter how small

### Safe Space Commitment
- No harsh criticism or mockery
- Everyone starts as a beginner
- Mistakes are learning opportunities
- Support over competition
